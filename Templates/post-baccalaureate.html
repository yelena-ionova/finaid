Apply for Aid

  • US Citizens & Permanent Residents
    • Complete FAFSA.
    • Complete Finaid/COLS Application at UCSF Financial Aid Portal. A username and password will be sent to you via e-mail.
    • E-sign Federal Direct Loan Master Promissory Note at Federal Student Aid. If you are interested in funding up to the total cost of attendance, including living expenses, you may also apply for the Graduate PLUS loans.
    • Complete Entrance Loan Counseling.
  • International Students
    • Complete Finaid/COLS Application at UCSF Financial Aid Portal. A username and password will be sent to you via e-mail.
    • Choose a private lender. Your choice is not limited to those institutions on our list. You may choose any lender you like that offers private educational loans. The borrower benefits listed online on the day the lender pre-approves your loan will apply to you.

2017-2018 Disbursement

  • Fall funds are available by August 17, 2017
  • Winter funds are available by January 10, 2018
  • Spring funds are available by April 3, 2018

In order to receive your funds, all students are required to sign-up for Electronic Fund Transfer (EFT). Failure to sign-up may result in the delays and inconveniences associated with mailing. To sign-up, you must:

  • Register to receive Direct (Stafford) and Graduate PLUS Direct Deposit
  • Complete Student Direct Deposit Request Form.

NOTE: Direct deposit of financial aid to your bank account is available to all financial aid, including Direct (Stafford) and Graduate Plus Loans! We recommend choosing the bank account EFT option to receive funds. Choosing the debit card option will involve incurring additional costs if you intend to use the card to pay your UCSF tuition/fee balance. Students must clear any financial aid check holds and register by the registration deadline for that semester in order to qualify for direct deposit of funds by the disbursement date.
